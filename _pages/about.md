I am Song-Xin Qu (Jack), **a dedicated AI Engineer and researcher** currently pursuing graduate studies at the **University of Science and Technology of China (USTC)**. Specializing in Large Language Models (LLMs), my work sits at the intersection of advanced AI research. I am passionate about advancing LLM capabilities—with a particular focus on model alignment, verifiable reinforcement learning (RLHF), and complex reasoning.

Technical Expertise
======
My research and engineering efforts are centered on advancing Large Language Models (LLMs) through rigorous alignment, domain-specific reasoning, and intelligent agent architectures. I specialize in bridging theoretical AI research with highly scalable system designs. My core expertise encompasses:

Advanced Model Alignment & Reinforcement Learning: Specializing in complex reasoning tasks through Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), and Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO). I have deep expertise in developing Verification-Aware Reward Models (VRM) and applying Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) to ensure rigorous model outputs.

Scientific Domain Adaptation & Data Engineering: Pioneering the integration of physical axioms into LLM training. I am experienced in constructing large-scale, physics-consistent datasets (e.g., for quantum mechanics) and implementing hybrid verification-based training methods to adapt foundational models for specialized scientific execution.

Scalable Training & Inference Systems: Optimizing distributed training pipelines across multi-GPU environments using PyTorch and DeepSpeed. I focus on optimizing scaling laws for model reasoning, utilizing parameter-efficient techniques (LoRA, QLoRA) and high-throughput inference engines (vLLM) to balance performance with computational efficiency.

Career Vision
======
I am driven to push the boundaries of artificial general intelligence by focusing on the core challenges of complex reasoning and rigorous model alignment. Whether developing scalable verification-aware reward systems or refining advanced reinforcement learning frameworks (such as RLVR and GRPO), I aim to build language models that demonstrate robust, logical, and verifiable reasoning across diverse, open-ended tasks. My long-term goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical alignment research and practical, autonomous AI systems, contributing to the open-source ecosystem while shaping the next generation of trustworthy and highly capable foundation models.
